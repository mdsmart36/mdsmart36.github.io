About

Thanks for stopping by!

I'm just getting starting in web development, even though my roots in computing go back over 30 years. Boy, have times changed!

As a middle school student, one of my teachers recommended me for a spring break computer camp being held at the local community college. We learned how to connect rotary dial telephone headsets to something called a "modulator / demodulator" (or modem, for short). Then we would dial a phone number to the campus computer system, and, voila, the computer could talk to each other! From there, we learned the basics of BASIC -- Beginner's All-purpose Symbolic Instruction Code, which told the computer what to do.

And so the story goes... What was once a novelty has now become a necessity. Computers are everywhere, and those who know how to utilize them for personal use, business, communication, entertainment, manufacturing, science and research, can benefit greatly.

After being in the ministry for 20 years, I'm circling back to coding. It's a hot topic right now, the demand for quality coders is great, and I want to be part of the story in shaping the future.
